Settings CTA
August 2026

Settings CTAs move from top to bottom

Settings actions leave the crowded top header and settle into the left-rail footer. Create and Ask stay up top; settings, notifications, and profile move down.

Why design needs this
  • Clear workspace. Top chrome stays focused on create/ask; account and system tools stop competing with page content.
  • Thumb- and habit-friendly. Settings, notifications, and profile live in a stable bottom-rail destination users already expect in modern app shells.
  • Scalable system actions. New utilities can join the bottom cluster without growing the header into a dense icon strip.

Drawer
August 2026

Drawer improvements

Drawers open with clearer hierarchy, tighter spacing, and smoother motion — easier to scan and act without losing context of the page behind.

Why design needs this
  • Faster orientation. Stronger title, actions, and content rhythm so users know what to do in one glance.
  • Less visual noise. Refined panels keep focus on the task instead of chrome and dense controls.
  • Motion with purpose. Enter/exit feel intentional and calm — aligned with the AI Native experience.

Typography
August 2026

Inter becomes the primary typeface

We're moving to Inter as the primary typeface across the entire dashboard. The previous mixed type stack is being retired.

Why design needs this
  • One voice. A single UI typeface keeps nav, headings, and body copy in the same rhythm.
  • Sharper hierarchy. Inter’s weights make dense dashboards easier to scan without increasing font size.
  • AI Native alignment. A modern, consistent typeface keeps the dashboard visually continuous with the next product tier.
